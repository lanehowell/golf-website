Four Rules This Program Follows

1
Hip drives everything. All lower body work emphasizes the right hip (your lead hip as a left-hander) clearing through a full range. If you feel lower back fatigue during any exercise, reduce weight and focus on glute engagement first.
2
Rotational exercises are done in both directions, every time. Your swing only goes one way — the gym should balance that out to prevent asymmetric overuse on the right side of your spine.
3
Power before fatigue. Rotational cable work always comes before isolation work. Never do heavy cable choppers when your core is already exhausted.
4
Progress weight slowly on any exercise where you feel it in your lower back instead of your glutes or core. That's a sign the posterior chain hasn't caught up yet.

Day 1 · ~45 min

Power & Rotation

Rotational power and posterior chain. The heaviest day for the movements that directly translate to swing speed.

Power Day — Cable Machine Primary
Session Progress 0 / 0 exercises
Warmup · 5 min · no rest
01
Cable Thoracic Rotation
T-spine mobility · Warmup
2×10
each side
▼
Cable at chest height. Stand perpendicular. Both hands on handle, hips stay square — rotate only the upper body. Slow and controlled. This is prep, not a workout.
Golf TipTrains X-factor: the separation between shoulder turn and hip turn that generates lag.
02
Cable Pull-Through
Hip hinge · Glute activation
1×12
light
▼
Cable low, face away. Straddle the cable, hinge at hips, handle between legs. Drive hips forward to standing — squeeze the glutes hard at the top. This is warmup weight — just wake up the pattern.
Golf TipThis hip drive pattern is exactly the thrust that happens through impact. Get comfortable with it before loading it.
Back Pain NoteIf you feel this in your lower back instead of your glutes, push your hips back further before each rep. The hinge, not the spine, does the work.
Main Work · 35 min · 60s rest between sets
Superset A — Complete A1 + A2 back to back · 3 rounds · 75s rest after each round
03
Cable Low-to-High Chop
Rotational power · Downswing plane
3×8
each side
▼
Cable at ankle height. Stand perpendicular, golf-width stance. Pull handle from low (lead hip level) diagonally to high (opposite shoulder). Initiate with the hips — the arms follow. Do 2–3 slow control reps then add power bursts. Lead side first.
Golf TipMaps directly to your downswing plane. The hip-first initiation is the kinematic sequence you're training your nervous system to repeat.
04
Cable High-to-Low Chop
Deceleration · Follow-through
3×8
each side
▼
Cable high. Same stance, same rotation — but from high (trail shoulder) to low (lead hip). Control the deceleration — don't let the cable snap you back. The eccentric is where back injuries happen.
Golf TipTrains your lead side to decelerate the club after impact. Weak deceleration = loss of control and stress on the lower back.
Superset B — Complete B1 + B2 back to back · 3 rounds · 60s rest after each round
05
Cable Pallof Press
Anti-rotation · Lumbar stability
3×10
each side · 1s hold
▼
Cable at chest height. Stand perpendicular, feet shoulder-width. Hold handle at sternum. Press straight out, hold 1 second, return. The entire point is resisting the cable pulling you sideways — keep hips and shoulders perfectly square.
Golf TipBuilds the deep stabilizers that protect the lumbar spine under rotational load. Weak here = flipping at impact and back pain.
Back Pain NoteThis is the single most important exercise for your lower back specifically. Prioritize it even on days you're not feeling 100%.
06
Cable Pull-Through (working weight)
Glute power · Hip drive
3×10
—
▼
Same as warmup but with real weight now. Focus on an explosive hip drive forward and a full glute squeeze at lockout. Hinge back with control. If you feel it in your lower back, drop the weight.
Back Pain NoteGlute strength is the most direct fix for chronic golf lower back pain. When the glutes fire properly, the erectors stop overworking.
Finisher · Single sets · 45s rest
07
Cable Face Pull
Rotator cuff · Rear delt · Posture
3×15
30s rest
▼
Rope attachment, cable at face height. Pull to forehead, elbows flaring high and wide. At the end, externally rotate so thumbs point behind you. Light weight, high reps. Never skip this.
Golf TipProtects the shoulder from thousands of rotational swings. Also counteracts the forward rounding that golfers develop over years of play.
08
Cable External Rotation
Rotator cuff · Trail shoulder
2×15
each side
▼
Cable at hip height. Elbow bent 90°, pinned to your side. Rotate forearm outward against the cable. Very light weight — this is joint maintenance. Slow and deliberate.
Golf TipThe external rotators must eccentrically control the downswing on every rep. Keep them strong or shoulder injuries follow.
Day 2 · ~50 min

Strength & Stability

General strength through compound movements. Builds the structural foundation that supports everything else — and protects the back over the long term.

Strength Day — Smith Machine + Dumbbells
Session Progress 0 / 0 exercises
Warmup · 5 min
01
Treadmill / Bike Warmup
Cardio warmup · Blood flow
5 min
moderate pace
▼
Treadmill at a brisk walk (3.5–4 mph) or bike at moderate resistance. Not a workout — just getting blood flowing and core temperature up before loading the spine with compound lifts.
Main Work · 40 min
02
Smith Machine Romanian Deadlift
Posterior chain · Hamstrings · Glutes
4×8
90s rest
▼
Bar at hip height. Push hips back, keep a long neutral spine — no rounding. Lower bar to mid-shin, feel the hamstring stretch, drive hips forward to stand. The Smith guide rails make this safer for the lower back while you build strength.
Golf TipStrong hamstrings and glutes are the #1 long-term fix for golf lower back pain. Every pound you add here matters.
Back Pain NoteIf you feel compression in the lower back at any point, you've lost neutral spine. Reset and reduce weight.
03
Smith Machine Incline Press
Upper chest · Anterior shoulder · Pushing strength
3×10
75s rest
▼
Bench at 30–45°. Moderate grip width. Lower bar to upper chest with control, press up without fully locking out. Keep shoulder blades pinched together throughout — this protects the shoulder joint.
Golf TipUpper body pressing strength supports the lead arm structure through the swing and reduces fatigue during long rounds.
Superset C — Complete C1 + C2 back to back · 3 rounds · 60s rest
04
Single-Leg DB Romanian Deadlift
Hip stability · Lateral glute · Balance
3×8
each leg
▼
DB in opposite hand from working leg. Hinge at hip, rear leg floats back. Keep hips square — resist the urge to rotate open. Right leg especially: focus on keeping the right hip from flaring. Use the Smith machine bar for light balance assist as needed.
Golf TipYour follow-through is a single-leg balance. This builds exactly that. Right leg is your lead leg — give it extra attention.
Back Pain NoteRight lateral glute weakness is likely a key contributor to your right-side back pain. This is the exercise to fix it.
05
Fly Machine (Chest Fly)
Chest · Shoulder stability
3×12
—
▼
Adjust seat so handles are at chest height. Control the opening slowly (2–3 seconds) and squeeze on the close. Don't force the full range if the shoulder doesn't feel right — stop where it's comfortable.
Superset D — Complete D1 + D2 back to back · 3 rounds · 60s rest
06
DB Lateral Raise
Medial delt · Shoulder width
3×12
—
▼
Light DBs. Raise to just below shoulder height with a slight forward tilt. Lead with your elbows, not your wrists. Control the descent — don't drop them. Avoid shrugging the traps up.
07
DB Hammer Curl
Biceps · Forearm · Grip
3×12
—
▼
Neutral grip (thumbs up). Curl with control, full range. Alternate arms or do both simultaneously. The neutral grip hits the brachialis and forearm more than a standard curl — both of which matter for club control.
Golf TipForearm and grip strength directly correlates to clubhead speed and late-round endurance.
Finisher
08
DB Farmer's Carry
Grip endurance · Shoulder stability · Core
2×40s
45s rest
▼
Heavy DBs. Walk slowly and deliberately. Shoulders back and down — don't shrug. Squeeze the handles hard. Stop before your grip fails. Every second of this is building grip endurance for holes 15–18.
Day 3 · ~45 min

Lower Body & Hip Power

The hip-focused day. Leg press loads the lower body without spinal compression. Finishes with cardio conditioning to build the walking endurance for 18 holes.

Hip Day — Leg Press + Cable + Cardio
Session Progress 0 / 0 exercises
Warmup · 5 min
01
Cable Hip Circle
Hip mobility · Joint prep
2×10
each side
▼
Cable low, ankle attachment or hold handle. Stand on one leg, trace a slow deliberate circle with the other hip — forward, out, back, return. Right hip gets extra attention. Keep the standing leg soft.
Back Pain NoteRight hip restriction is likely your #1 back pain driver. This warmup is therapeutic — do it on non-gym days too.
02
Stationary Bike Warmup
Hip flexor warmup · Blood flow
5 min
easy pace
▼
Easy resistance, smooth pedaling. Focus on a full hip circle — push through the top, pull through the bottom. This primes the hip flexors and glutes before loading them on the leg press.
Main Work · 30 min
03
Leg Press (Wide Stance)
Quads · Glutes · Hip drive
4×10
90s rest
▼
Feet shoulder-width or slightly wider, toes angled out 15–20°. Lower to 90° — don't let the lower back peel off the seat. Press through the heels. Wide stance emphasizes the glutes and inner thigh — both key for hip stability.
Golf TipLeg press lets you load the lower body heavily without compressing the spine — ideal when the back is already irritated.
Back Pain NoteIf your lower back peels off the pad at any point in the range, you've gone too deep. Adjust your foot position up the plate slightly.
04
Single-Leg Leg Press
Unilateral strength · Hip balance
3×8
each leg · 60s rest
▼
One foot centered on the plate. Lighter weight than bilateral. Notice if the right leg (your lead leg) is noticeably weaker — that's common and worth addressing. Focus on pushing through the heel and keeping the hip from rotating.
Back Pain NoteBilateral leg strength imbalances between right and left often show up as asymmetric back pain. Tracking this over weeks tells you if you're closing the gap.
Superset E — Complete E1 + E2 back to back · 3 rounds · 60s rest
05
Cable Low-to-High Chop (power focus)
Rotational power · Speed
3×6
each side · explosive
▼
Moderate weight. All 6 reps are power bursts — maximum intent and speed. This is not a controlled exercise today. Let the hip fire first and the arms follow. Rest fully between sides.
Golf TipSwing speed is a skill trained at maximal intent. These fast-twitch reps on a rested core are more valuable for speed than 20 tired slow reps.
06
Cable Pallof Press
Anti-rotation · Back protection
3×10
each side · 1s hold
▼
Same as Day 1. Press out, hold, return. Hips square. This pairs with the power choppers to train both rotational power AND rotational stability in the same session.
Cardio Finisher · 10 min
07
Treadmill Walk (incline)
Course endurance · Active recovery
10 min
3–5% incline
▼
3.5–4 mph, 3–5% incline. This simulates walking hilly fairways. Not a sprint — this is active recovery and aerobic conditioning. Keep posture tall and avoid hunching over the handrails.
Golf TipWalking 18 holes is 4–6 miles. This builds the endurance to stay strong on the back nine when most handicap strokes are lost.
Every Day · Including Rest Days

Daily Mobility Routine

8–10 minutes. Do this every morning or before any round of golf. The right hip and thoracic spine work is the most direct intervention for your lower back pain — consistency here matters more than any single workout.

Best time: Morning before you get stiff, or 30 min before teeing off. Order matters — do them in sequence. Tap each stretch for full instructions and hold times.
Hip & Glute · Right side gets extra attention
01
Figure-Four (Piriformis) Stretch
Right hip · Piriformis · SI joint
60–90seach side
▼
Standing version: Cross your right ankle over your left knee. Sit back into a partial squat, holding a wall or rack for balance. Feel the deep stretch in the right glute. Right side first, longer hold.

Floor version: Lie on back, cross right ankle over left knee, pull left thigh toward chest.

Hold 60s right side, 45s left side. Breathe deeply — exhale into the stretch.
02
90/90 Hip Internal Rotation
Right hip internal rotation · Lead hip mobility
60seach side
▼
Sit on the floor, both knees bent at 90°. Front leg shin faces forward, back leg shin faces behind. Sit tall. Gently rotate your torso toward your front leg, trying to get your chest over your front knee.

Right hip internal rotation (right leg in front) is the movement your lead hip needs through impact. This is one of the highest-value stretches in this routine. Hold and breathe — don't force it.
03
Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch
Hip flexors · Psoas · Lumbar tension
45seach side
▼
Lunge position, back knee on ground (use a mat or folded towel). Tuck your tailbone under slightly — this is key, most people miss it. Shift your weight forward until you feel the stretch in the front of the back leg's hip. Keep your torso upright.

Tight hip flexors pull directly on the lumbar spine. This stretch is often the fastest source of immediate lower back relief.
Thoracic Spine · T-spine rotation is your back's best protection
04
Thoracic Rotation (Standing)
T-spine mobility · Swing separation
10 repseach direction
▼
Stand in golf posture — slight hip hinge, arms crossed over chest. Rotate your upper body only, keeping your hips pointing forward. Go to comfortable end range, pause, return. Gradually increase range over the reps.

Do 5 slow controlled reps each way, then 5 slightly faster to groove the pattern.
05
Cat-Cow (Spinal Mobility)
Lumbar decompression · Spine hydration
10 repsslow breath
▼
On hands and knees. Inhale: let the belly drop, lift the head and tailbone (cow). Exhale: round the spine up toward the ceiling, tuck chin and tailbone (cat). Move slowly with the breath — don't rush this.

This is spinal decompression in motion. Particularly valuable first thing in the morning before the discs fully load with the day's activity.
Hamstrings & Posterior Chain
06
Standing Hamstring Stretch
Hamstrings · Sciatic nerve · Address posture
45seach side
▼
Place your heel on a bench or low surface. Hinge at the hip with a straight back — don't round forward. You should feel it behind the knee and up the hamstring. Flex your foot (toes up) to deepen the stretch slightly.

Tight hamstrings pull the pelvis into posterior tilt, which flattens the lumbar curve and increases back stress through the swing. This is directly related to your back pain.
07
Lateral Hip Shift (IT Band / Glute Med)
Lateral hip · IT band · Right glute med
30seach side
▼
Stand next to a wall, near side hand on the wall for balance. Cross your far leg behind the near leg. Push your near hip toward the wall — you'll feel the lateral hip and IT band stretch. Right side especially.

The glute medius (lateral hip muscle) is frequently the weakest link in golfers with right-side back pain — it stops controlling the pelvis and the back picks up the slack.
Wrists & Forearms · 2 minutes total
08
Wrist Flexor + Extensor Stretch
Forearm · Elbow health · Grip prep
30sboth directions
▼
Flexor: Arm extended, palm up. Use other hand to gently pull fingers down toward the floor. Feel it along the forearm underside.

Extensor: Arm extended, palm down. Use other hand to gently push the back of the hand down. Feel it on the forearm topside.

Do both directions, both arms. This takes 2 minutes and prevents golfer's and tennis elbow — common in dedicated golfers.
